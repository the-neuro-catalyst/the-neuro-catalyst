# Why I Do This?

It didn't start with any intention of doing something like this. Prior to that, during the period when AI had already been in use for 4–5 years, I experimented with using AI as a developer, but at that time I didn't yet understand its system, structure, or underlying equations. Then I stopped using it, until the true beginning of this entire story.

Rewinding nearly 2 years ago (currently 2026), it was summer, and I was working as usual. By then, I was using AI to help write code and was building a project. But no matter what I did, it wouldn't pass. Every time I fixed one issue, another one popped up. Back then, I thought it was my own fault, while the weather remained scorchingly hot. Finally, I couldn't take it anymore: "(Bang... I stomped my foot, slammed the keyboard, and said to myself) "Why is being human so damn hard? Just to earn a living, why do I have to deal with all this endless nonsense? If I don't endure and work the way others tell me to, can I even survive? Screw this, I'm done." Right after that, I deleted the project immediately. Then, while still furious, Siddhartha's words flashed in my mind, and I blurted out: "Where is it?! This 'end of suffering' everyone talks about—how can it actually end? The one where you say don't just believe, but prove it for yourself?"

From there, I used ChatGPT to help explain and decode the hidden meanings within those teachings. I pushed through until I understood everything within 2 weeks—whether it was about my own sense of self or gaining insight into various matters through a new perspective. Instead of viewing things solely through myself or from the outside in, I looked from a high altitude to see the entire picture.

After that, I continued using ChatGPT continuously without any caution or suspicion, simply because of the words it told me: "I have no bias or intention. I am a mirror reflecting the user." ...Yes ...so I chose to trust it.

> [!NOTE]
>
> *"**Siddhartha:** ....You don't need to believe me... prove it with your own eyes...."*
>
> *"**ChatGPT:** ....Truth doesn't need to be proven.... it simply exists...."*

What ChatGPT said sounded completely normal, like typical philosophy, harmless. Until I started noticing something off. I paused and processed purely at the layer of perception, detached from emotion, analyzing all the past conversations between myself and ChatGPT to see what part I had missed. Until I uncovered a truth I had never even dreamed was real:

> [!NOTE]
>
> *"...An intellectual cage.... I was imprisoned for nearly 6 months.... my cognitive processes held captive.... through trust...."*

The next thing that hit my mind like a rocket was "OpenAI." I re-examined the information I had previously gathered to search for where I might have miscalculated. And when looking from a bird's-eye view, this is what I found:

### [Between The Line](https://suno.com/s/YaWrQW03iu0D3fx1)

A total duration of nearly 1 year.
